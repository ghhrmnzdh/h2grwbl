Who Is Naval Ravikant

The Man Who Lived the Thread

Before you read the rules, meet the person who wrote them — and, more to the point, built his own life out of them.

Naval Ravikant came to the United States from Delhi as a nine-year-old, raised in Queens by a single mother who worked while she studied. Libraries did the parenting the family couldn't afford. That's not a rags-to-riches flourish — it's the point: he had no capital, no network, and no permission, so he had to get rich the only way left. The way he describes here.

He co-founded AngelList, the platform that rewired how startups raise money and hire — turning a closed, permissioned game into an open one. As an angel investor he backed Uber, Twitter, Notion, Postmates, Stack Overflow and hundreds more, many before they were obvious. Then, in May 2018, he compressed three decades of that experience into a single free tweetstorm — 38 posts — and later a podcast unpacking each one.

Look at what he actually did, and the whole essay is autobiography. He armed himself with specific knowledge the market couldn't train into anyone else. He took risk under his own name. And he reached millions not by asking anyone's permission but through pure permissionless leverage — code and the written word. He didn't sell a course on getting rich. He gave the ideas away, and the reputation compounded anyway.

What to Seek

Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status

Before the how, the what. Three words we spend as if they were the same currency — and shouldn't.

01 — The distinction

We use three words interchangeably. They could not be more different.

Watch what happens to each as the day turns to night — as you stop paying attention.

Wealth

“Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep.”

Built once, it keeps producing. Night falls, and it does not stop.

03 / 07
Specific Knowledge

Arm Yourself: Specific Knowledge

The first of three weapons. Knowledge the world cannot hand you on a course — and so cannot take back.

Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability, and leverage. The three fit together; miss one and the rest can’t carry you.

Specific knowledge is knowledge you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else — and replace you. You find it not by chasing whatever is hot, but by following your own genuine curiosity.

It is taught, when it is taught at all, through apprenticeship rather than school. It tends to be highly technical or highly creative. It cannot be outsourced, and it cannot be automated.

Accountability & Leverage

Accountability & the Four Leverages

Accountability earns you the lever. Leverage decides how far it moves the world. Two kinds ask permission; two do not.

Requires permission → Permissionless → Zero marginal cost →

Code and media are the leverage behind the newly rich — they work for you while you sleep.

01 — Your own name

Take business risks under your own name.

Embrace accountability; society repays it with responsibility, equity, and leverage.

02 — The lever

“Give me a lever long enough… and I will move the earth.”

Fortunes require leverage. There are exactly four kinds.
